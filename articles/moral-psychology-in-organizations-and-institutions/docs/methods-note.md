# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model ethical sensitivity, role constraint, cultural climate, performance pressure, hierarchy intensity, diffusion of responsibility, accountability strength, speak-up protection, value alignment, corruption pressure, unethical action probability, and institutional integrity.

## Core methodological claim

Moral psychology in organizations should be modeled as an interaction between persons and systems. Ethical behavior is shaped by character, conscience, identity, and empathy, but also by roles, incentives, hierarchy, procedures, culture, and institutional design.

## Modeled constructs

- ethical sensitivity
- role constraint
- cultural climate
- performance pressure
- hierarchy intensity
- diffusion of responsibility
- accountability strength
- speak-up protection
- value alignment
- corruption pressure
- unethical action probability
- institutional integrity

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset models unethical action risk as a logistic function of organizational pressure variables and ethical dampening variables. Risk rises with performance pressure, hierarchy, diffusion of responsibility, and corruption pressure. Risk falls with accountability strength, cultural climate, speak-up protection, and value alignment.

Institutional integrity is modeled separately as a continuous system-level indicator.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real employees, real organizations, real institutional integrity, or workplace ethics.
