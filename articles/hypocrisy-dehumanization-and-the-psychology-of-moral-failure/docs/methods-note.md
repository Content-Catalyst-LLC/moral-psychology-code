# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model judgment strictness toward others, judgment strictness toward self, hypocrisy, perceived humanness, dehumanization, ingroup bias, empathic recognition, public value claims, actual practice integrity, institutional hypocrisy, accountability strength, victim visibility, and moral failure probability.

## Core methodological claim

Hypocrisy and dehumanization should be modeled as distinct but mutually reinforcing mechanisms of moral failure. Hypocrisy measures asymmetry in standards. Dehumanization measures reduction in perceived moral standing. Institutional hypocrisy measures the gap between public value claims and actual practice integrity.

## Modeled constructs

- judgment strictness toward others
- judgment strictness toward self or ingroup
- hypocrisy
- perceived humanness
- dehumanization
- ingroup bias
- empathic recognition
- public value claim
- actual practice integrity
- institutional hypocrisy
- accountability strength
- victim visibility
- moral failure probability

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset generates case-level moral asymmetry and moral exclusion variables and then derives moral failure probability. The model intentionally allows moral failure risk to increase through more than one pathway: double standards, dehumanization, group favoritism, institutional value-practice gaps, weak accountability, and low victim visibility.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real people, organizations, institutions, political groups, communities, or conflicts.
