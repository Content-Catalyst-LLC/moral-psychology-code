# Validation Plan

This scaffold is synthetic and conceptual. Validation focuses on reproducibility, interpretive clarity, and responsible use.

## Checks

1. Scripts run from the article root folder.
2. Output folders are created automatically.
3. Generated tables are written to `outputs/tables`.
4. Figure outputs are written to `outputs/figures` where applicable.
5. Synthetic cases are not interpreted as real persons, organizations, political groups, institutions, or communities.
6. Hypocrisy score is not interpreted as a real-person moral diagnosis.
7. Dehumanization score is not interpreted as a real group or individual assessment.
8. Institutional hypocrisy is not interpreted as an institutional audit.
9. Moral failure probability is not interpreted as a prediction of real conduct.
10. Outputs are not used for employee discipline, political targeting, moderation, surveillance, psychological assessment, legal decisions, or automated moral judgment.

## Possible empirical extensions

Future empirical work could include:

- moral hypocrisy experiments
- comparable-case judgment studies
- dehumanization and moral exclusion measures
- ingroup/outgroup moral judgment studies
- institutional values-practice gap audits
- language analysis of dehumanizing rhetoric
- victim visibility and testimony studies
- accountability and whistleblowing datasets
- public virtue and organizational ethics research
- political hypocrisy and moral comparability studies
- bureaucratic distance and administrative harm research

Any empirical extension should include source documentation, privacy safeguards, ethical review where appropriate, measurement validation, and careful interpretation.
