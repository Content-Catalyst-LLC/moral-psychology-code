# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model harm sensitivity, fairness sensitivity, loyalty sensitivity, authority sensitivity, purity sensitivity, relational obligation, norm learning, sanction expectation, moral judgment, and norm-enforcement tendency across placeholder cultural contexts.

## Core methodological claim

Cross-cultural moral psychology should be modeled as structured variation in moral weighting and interpretation, not as either total sameness or total incomparability. Shared moral dimensions can receive different cultural weights, producing different moral judgments and enforcement patterns.

## Modeled constructs

- cultural context
- harm sensitivity
- fairness sensitivity
- loyalty sensitivity
- authority sensitivity
- purity sensitivity
- relational obligation
- norm learning
- sanction expectation
- moral judgment
- norm-enforcement tendency
- cultural moral-profile variation

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset includes three placeholder contexts: `Context_A`, `Context_B`, and `Context_C`. These are not real cultures. They are used to demonstrate how moral judgment can vary when shared moral dimensions are weighted differently across contexts.

`Context_A` places stronger synthetic emphasis on harm and fairness. `Context_B` places stronger synthetic emphasis on loyalty, authority, purity, and relational obligation. `Context_C` uses a mixed profile.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real cultures, nations, religions, political communities, ethnic groups, or institutions.
