# Validation Plan

This scaffold is synthetic and conceptual. Validation focuses on reproducibility, interpretive clarity, and responsible use.

## Checks

1. Scripts run from the article root folder.
2. Output folders are created automatically.
3. Generated tables are written to `outputs/tables`.
4. Figure outputs are written to `outputs/figures` where applicable.
5. Synthetic cases are not interpreted as real persons, students, employees, leaders, organizations, institutions, cultures, political groups, religious communities, or communities.
6. Wrongness and blame outputs are not interpreted as real moral truth or moral worth.
7. Constructive response outputs are not used as automated intervention recommendations.
8. High-pressure high-judgment, low-repair high-blame, and wrongness-blame divergence cases are not real-person classifications.
9. Outputs are not used for employee screening, hiring, promotion, discipline, student assessment, workplace surveillance, psychological assessment, legal decisions, or automated moral judgment.
10. Article-level outputs remain clearly labeled as synthetic demonstration data.

## Possible empirical extensions

Future empirical work could include:

- moral judgment experiments
- wrongness and blame distinction studies
- intention-attribution tasks
- social pressure and group framing experiments
- digital public condemnation datasets
- institutional accountability and repair datasets
- apology, restitution, and repair research
- moral emotion and condemnation studies
- cross-cultural moral judgment studies
- reasoning-action gap studies

Any empirical extension should include source documentation, privacy safeguards, ethical review where appropriate, measurement validation, and careful interpretation.
