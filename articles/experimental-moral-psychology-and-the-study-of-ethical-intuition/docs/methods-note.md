# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model intuition, reflection, norm sensitivity, consequence sensitivity, intention, excuse, power condition, and blame in experimental moral psychology.

## Core methodological claim

Ethical intuition should be treated as one component in a larger moral-judgment system. Experimental designs can isolate pieces of that system, but no single task captures the whole of ethical life.

## Modeled constructs

- intuition strength
- reflection strength
- norm sensitivity
- consequence sensitivity
- intentionality
- excuse strength
- group identity salience
- power condition
- moral judgment
- blame probability
- blame assignment
- intuition/reflection gap

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset includes three power conditions:

1. `low_power`
2. `control`
3. `high_power`

The model uses synthetic variables to represent judgment-relevant components and then generates moral judgment and blame outcomes.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real moral agents.
