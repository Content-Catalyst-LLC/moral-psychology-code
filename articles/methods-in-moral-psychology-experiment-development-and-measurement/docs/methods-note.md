# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model experiment, development, and measurement in moral psychology.

## Core methodological claim

Moral psychology is methodologically plural by necessity. No single method captures the full structure of moral life. Experiments provide causal leverage, developmental designs show emergence and change, and measurement work clarifies what the field is actually studying.

## Modeled constructs

- wrongness judgment
- blame judgment
- norm learning
- reflection
- social desirability
- action tendency
- latent moral construct

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset includes three experimental conditions:

1. `control`
2. `intent_salient`
3. `excuse_salient`

It also includes an age variable from 8 to 70 to represent developmental and lifespan variation.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate methodological logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real moral agents.
