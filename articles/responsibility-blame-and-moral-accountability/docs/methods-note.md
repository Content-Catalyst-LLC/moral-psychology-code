# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model control, knowledge, role duty, wrongness severity, motive malice, mitigating excuse, institutional structure, victim recognition, repair capacity, prevention capacity, responsibility, blame probability, accountability score, and accountability quality.

## Core methodological claim

Responsibility, blame, and accountability should be modeled as distinct but connected constructs. Responsibility depends on control, knowledge, and role duty. Blame depends on responsibility, wrongness severity, motive, and mitigating excuse. Accountability depends on responsibility, institutional structure, repair capacity, prevention capacity, and the broader conditions under which answerability is enacted.

## Modeled constructs

- control
- knowledge
- role duty
- wrongness severity
- motive malice
- mitigating excuse
- institutional structure
- victim recognition
- repair capacity
- prevention capacity
- responsibility
- blame probability
- accountability score
- accountability quality

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset generates case-level variables and then derives responsibility, blame probability, accountability score, and accountability quality. The model intentionally allows blame and accountability to diverge. Some cases may involve high accountability needs even when blame is mitigated by excuse or constrained control.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real persons, legal cases, organizations, institutions, employees, public officials, or professional responsibility systems.
