# Validation Plan

This scaffold is synthetic and conceptual. Validation focuses on reproducibility, interpretive clarity, and responsible use.

## Checks

1. Scripts run from the article root folder.
2. Output folders are created automatically.
3. Generated tables are written to `outputs/tables`.
4. Figure outputs are written to `outputs/figures` where applicable.
5. Synthetic cases are not interpreted as real children, adolescents, families, schools, cultures, or communities.
6. Developmental band is not interpreted as a clinical or developmental classification.
7. Moral-development profile is not interpreted as a real child or adolescent score.
8. Moral action probability is not interpreted as a prediction of real conduct.
9. Peer-status pressure is not interpreted as a real peer risk score.
10. Outputs are not used for child assessment, school assessment, discipline, developmental screening, psychological assessment, legal decisions, educational placement, or automated moral judgment.

## Possible empirical extensions

Future empirical work could include:

- longitudinal childhood and adolescent moral development data
- perspective-taking and theory-of-mind measures
- empathy and prosocial behavior measures
- social-domain judgment tasks
- school climate and restorative-practice datasets
- family discipline and moral socialization studies
- peer-status and bullying datasets
- digital moral learning and online peer pressure studies
- adolescent moral identity measures
- cultural and cross-national developmental comparisons
- institutional discipline and student voice research
- inclusive curriculum and moral belonging studies

Any empirical extension should include source documentation, privacy safeguards, ethical review, child-protection protocols where appropriate, measurement validation, and careful interpretation.
