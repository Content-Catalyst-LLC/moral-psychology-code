# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model harm weighting, fairness weighting, loyalty weighting, purity weighting, authority weighting, care weighting, identity strength, framing sensitivity, institutional trust, pluralist tolerance, moral judgment, and disagreement distance.

## Core methodological claim

Moral disagreement should be modeled as structured divergence in value weighting, identity, framing, and trust rather than as random noise. This does not mean every disagreement is equally defensible. It means disagreement may emerge from different moral saliences and social-psychological frameworks before overt argument begins.

- harm weighting
- fairness weighting

- care weighting

- framing sensitivity

- pluralist tolerance
- moral judgment
- disagreement distance
- value gap
- identity gap
- framing gap
- trust gap

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset generates participant-level moral profiles and then samples pairwise comparisons. Disagreement is modeled as the absolute distance between moral judgments. Pair-level predictors include value gaps, identity gaps, framing gaps, and institutional-trust gaps.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real persons, political groups, cultures, religions, institutions, or populations.
