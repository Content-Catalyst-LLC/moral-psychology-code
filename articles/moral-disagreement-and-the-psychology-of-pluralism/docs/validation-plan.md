# Validation Plan

This scaffold is synthetic and conceptual. Validation focuses on reproducibility, interpretive clarity, and responsible use.

## Checks

1. Scripts run from the article root folder.
2. Output folders are created automatically.
3. Generated tables are written to `outputs/tables`.
4. Figure outputs are written to `outputs/figures` where applicable.
5. Synthetic participants are not interpreted as real people.
6. Disagreement distance is documented as a simplified analytic indicator.
7. Moral judgment scores are not interpreted as assessments of real moral character.
8. Value gaps and identity gaps are not used for profiling, targeting, moderation, or persuasion.
9. Code comments and docs clearly identify the data as synthetic.
10. Outputs are not used for ideological scoring, political profiling, surveillance, or decision-making.

## Possible empirical extensions

Future empirical work could include:

- validated moral-foundation or value-pluralism survey instruments
- moral disagreement experiments
- institutional trust measures
- deliberative-democracy datasets
- cross-cultural moral disagreement studies
- qualitative interviews about value conflict
- political identity and moral salience data
- public reasoning and tolerance studies
- procedural legitimacy and rights-protection measures
- longitudinal polarization and pluralism studies

Any empirical extension should include source documentation, privacy safeguards, ethical review where appropriate, measurement validation, and careful interpretation.
