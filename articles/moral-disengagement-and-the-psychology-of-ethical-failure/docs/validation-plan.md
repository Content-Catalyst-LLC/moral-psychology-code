# Validation Plan

This scaffold is synthetic and conceptual. Validation focuses on reproducibility, interpretive clarity, and responsible use.

## Checks

1. Scripts run from the article root folder.
2. Output folders are created automatically.
3. Generated tables are written to `outputs/tables`.
4. Figure outputs are written to `outputs/figures` where applicable.
5. Synthetic cases are not interpreted as real people, workplaces, institutions, organizations, or communities.
6. Moral disengagement is not interpreted as a real-person moral diagnosis.
7. Unethical action probability is not interpreted as a prediction of real conduct.
8. Ethical failure risk is not interpreted as an employment, legal, or disciplinary risk score.
9. Code comments and docs clearly identify the data as synthetic.
10. Outputs are not used for employee screening, workplace surveillance, misconduct prediction, disciplinary decisions, content moderation, psychological assessment, legal decisions, political targeting, or automated moral judgment.

## Possible empirical extensions

Future empirical work could include:

- moral disengagement scale validation
- organizational ethics and misconduct studies
- workplace accountability datasets
- consequence visibility and victim testimony studies
- dehumanization and victim blaming measures
- euphemistic language and organizational communication analysis
- leadership authorization and responsibility diffusion studies
- anticipated guilt and self-sanction research
- institutional pressure and ethical decision-making experiments
- ethical climate and psychological safety surveys

Any empirical extension should include source documentation, privacy safeguards, ethical review where appropriate, measurement validation, and careful interpretation.
