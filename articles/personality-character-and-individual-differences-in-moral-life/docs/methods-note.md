# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model broad personality, character strength, moral identity, empathy, conscientious self-regulation, humility, self-interest bias, situational pressure, voice safety, accountability strength, moral response profile, and moral action probability.

## Core methodological claim

Personality, character, and individual differences should be modeled as plural, layered, and context-sensitive. Broad personality, morally valenced character, moral identity, empathy, self-regulation, humility, and self-interest bias all shape moral response, but their expression depends on situational pressure, voice safety, accountability, and institutional climate.

## Modeled constructs

- broad personality
- character strength
- moral identity
- empathy
- conscientious self-regulation
- humility
- self-interest bias
- situational pressure
- voice safety
- accountability strength
- moral response profile
- moral action probability
- person-situation interaction

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset generates case-level individual-difference and context variables, then derives a moral response profile and moral action probability. The model intentionally allows strong moral-response profiles to fail under high pressure or low voice safety. This reflects the article’s core argument that character is real but context-sensitive.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real people, employees, students, organizations, cultures, communities, political groups, or moral worth.
