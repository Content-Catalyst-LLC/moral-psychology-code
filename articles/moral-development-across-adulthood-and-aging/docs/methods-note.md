# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model moral development across adulthood and aging. It includes age, adulthood band, moral identity, prosocial orientation, role complexity, moral-emotional profile, decision capacity, generativity, caregiving burden, loss reflection, voice safety, accountability strength, latent moral-development profile, and moral action probability.

## Core methodological claim

Adult moral development should be modeled as a changing person-role-context process rather than as a finished endpoint of adolescence. Moral identity, prosocial orientation, role responsibility, moral emotion, decision capacity, generativity, burden, and institutional support all shape how moral commitments are expressed across adulthood and aging.

## Modeled constructs

- adult age
- adulthood band
- moral identity
- prosocial orientation
- role complexity
- moral-emotional profile
- decision capacity
- generativity
- caregiving burden
- loss reflection
- voice safety
- accountability strength
- latent moral-development profile
- moral action probability

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset generates adult lifespan observations and derives a latent moral-development profile. A second stage derives moral action probability as a function of the profile, voice safety, accountability strength, and caregiving burden. This design intentionally allows high-development low-action cases, where burden and weak support suppress moral expression despite a strong profile.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real adults, older adults, caregivers, families, workers, patients, institutions, cultures, or communities.
