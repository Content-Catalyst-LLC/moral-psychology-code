# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic data to model developmental moral reasoning, stage-like change, domain differentiation, justice reasoning, care reasoning, conventional sensitivity, autonomy orientation, high-complexity developmental profiles, and stage-domain mismatch cases.

## Core methodological claim

Moral reasoning develops over time, but not along one narrow universal staircase. Piaget and Kohlberg remain important because they made moral development structural, but contemporary moral psychology requires a multidimensional approach that includes justice, care, convention sensitivity, authority orientation, relationality, culture, peer cooperation, and domain differentiation.

- autonomy orientation
- high-complexity cases
- stage-domain mismatch cases

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset derives latent reasoning from age, perspective-taking, norm exposure, domain differentiation, peer cooperation, cultural support, and authority orientation. Justice reasoning, care reasoning, conventional sensitivity, and autonomy orientation are then modeled as related but distinct dimensions. Stage-like categories are generated from latent reasoning thresholds, while case profiles help demonstrate why stage labels should not be treated as complete descriptions of moral development.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real children, adolescents, students, schools, families, organizations, institutions, communities, cultures, religious communities, political groups, or moral worth.
