# Methods Note

This article scaffold uses synthetic historical-explanatory weights to model broad shifts in moral psychology from moral sense theory and sentimentalism to contemporary experimental, cultural, political, and institutional research.

## Core methodological claim

The history of moral psychology is best treated as a redistribution of explanatory emphasis rather than a simple sequence of replacements. Sentiment, reason, development, intuition, culture, neuroscience, experiment, and institutions all remain relevant, though their prominence changes across historical periods.

## Modeled dimensions

- sentiment and sympathy
- reason and principle
- development
- intuition
- culture and politics
- neuroscience and experiment
- institutions and structures
- dominant explanatory mode
- diversification index
- explanatory growth over time

## Synthetic design

The synthetic dataset assigns illustrative weights to major historical periods. These weights are not publication counts, citation data, archival measurements, author rankings, or disciplinary rankings. They are conceptual inputs for reproducible article demonstrations, visualizations, and teaching workflows.

## Interpretation

The generated data are synthetic. They should be used to demonstrate modeling logic, reproducibility, and article-level scaffolding. They should not be interpreted as empirical findings about real publication frequency, historical influence, disciplinary importance, or citation impact.
