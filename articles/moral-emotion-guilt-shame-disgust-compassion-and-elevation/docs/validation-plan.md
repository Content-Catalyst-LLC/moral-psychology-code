# Validation Plan

This scaffold is synthetic and conceptual. Validation focuses on reproducibility, interpretive clarity, and responsible use.

## Checks

1. Scripts run from the article root folder.
2. Output folders are created automatically.
3. Generated tables are written to `outputs/tables`.
4. Figure outputs are written to `outputs/figures` where applicable.
5. Synthetic cases are not interpreted as real persons, students, employees, leaders, organizations, institutions, cultures, political groups, religious communities, or communities.
6. Guilt, shame, disgust, compassion, and elevation are not interpreted as psychological or clinical assessments.
7. Helping, repair, and avoidance tendencies are not interpreted as predictions of real behavior.
8. Constructive and punitive emotion indexes are not interpreted as moral-worth, virtue, risk, or character scores.
9. Emotion-profile cases are not interpreted as real-person classifications.
10. Outputs are not used for employee screening, hiring, promotion, discipline, student assessment, workplace surveillance, psychological assessment, legal decisions, or automated moral judgment.

## Possible empirical extensions

Future empirical work could include:

- moral emotion scales
- guilt and shame measures
- disgust sensitivity and moral disgust research
- compassion and empathic concern datasets
- moral elevation studies
- helping and prosocial behavior experiments
- moral repair and apology data
- outrage and social media behavior data
- institutional emotional-climate research
- caregiver fatigue and compassion support data

Any empirical extension should include source documentation, privacy safeguards, ethical review where appropriate, measurement validation, and careful interpretation.
