# Validation Plan

This scaffold is synthetic and conceptual. Validation focuses on reproducibility, interpretive clarity, and responsible use.

## Checks

1. Scripts run from the article root folder.
2. Output folders are created automatically.
3. Generated tables are written to `outputs/tables`.
4. Figure outputs are written to `outputs/figures` where applicable.
5. Synthetic cases are not interpreted as real persons, policies, institutions, or communities.
6. Distributive judgment is not interpreted as a real fairness score.
7. Support for fair allocation is not interpreted as public opinion or policy preference.
8. Inequity aversion is not interpreted as psychological assessment.
9. Code comments and docs clearly identify the data as synthetic.
10. Outputs are not used for eligibility, welfare, compensation, benefits, hiring, policy allocation, political targeting, or automated justice decisions.

## Possible empirical extensions

Future empirical work could include:

- distributive justice survey instruments
- inequity-aversion experiments
- public goods and allocation games
- developmental fairness datasets
- procedural justice and institutional trust surveys
- need-based allocation experiments
- merit and desert judgment studies
- reparative justice and historical-memory surveys
- welfare-policy framing studies
- cross-cultural distributive justice data
- political ideology and redistribution datasets

Any empirical extension should include source documentation, privacy safeguards, ethical review where appropriate, measurement validation, and careful interpretation.
